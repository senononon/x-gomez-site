The Royals of Scandal

Science Fiction, Dystopian, Speculative, Political

Synopsis:
Peace there exists in the New World, a post-war monarchical society led by powerful families. House Swain, the liege lords of the north; House Ski, the matriarchs of the east; House Lawless, the lords of law; and House Cardinal, the custodians of the all-powerful Council. Soon peace falls as scandals arise. All while Icarus Swain attempts to reclaim his glory that never was.

The Royals of Scandal marks the rise of the New Delariarte, and the beginning of the Fall of the New World.

Prelude

The noval gland (royal gland) is a small organ located in the brain of Homo novusapiens, responsible for producing one of five distinct hormone types. It began as a mutated brain tumor that evolved into a functional gland, granting novusapiens various superhuman abilities based on the hormone it secretes, distinguishing them as a high-level species, superior to Homo sapiens.
Royals are born with a dormant noval gland, roughly the size of a pea. As the brain develops, the gland gradually grows, reaching full maturity by the age of seven and activating its potential. The specific hormone it secretes—and, by extension, the abilities it grants—is primarily determined by heredity.
But even the strongest bloodlines could not escape the hands of fate, just as fate itself was never one to keep its hands to itself.

Alwyn Lawless—“The Perjurist,” a man who can bring the truth out of someone by the touch of his hand—questions Damn Bytheriver—“The Murder,” a boy who births crows from his mouth.

A royal conclave summons the heads of all the houses in the New World.

The final part of a 3-chapter narrative run; Set in the Salt Hall of Fig Island, the hunters consummate their hunts and plan to draw the first blood.
